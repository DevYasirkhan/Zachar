Personal Injury FAQs

I’ve been injured in an accident in Arizona. How long do I have to file a claim?

In Arizona, the statute of limitations on personal injury is generally two years. This means that generally, claims filed more than two years after the date of the accident are likely to be dismissed. However, this is not a certainty. Some claims have as little as 180 days after the accident to do something, others, only one year. If you have a legal claim for injuries, DO NOT DELAY. Call Zachar Law Firm today to discuss your case.

In Arizona, who can file a wrongful death claim?

The state of Arizona limits the persons who are able to file a wrongful death claim. These are the surviving spouse of the deceased person, surviving children, a surviving parent, the personal representative of the deceased person or the personal representative of the deceased person’s estate.

Is there a limit to the amount of compensation I can receive?

In Arizona, there are no limits. Some states have “damage caps” (limitations on the amount of damages one can receive). Arizona has no damage caps.

What kind of compensation am I entitled to in a personal injury case? What am I owed in a wrongful death claim?

Claimants in personal injury and wrongful death cases are able to recover both financial and nonfinancial damages. Examples of financial damages include medical bills, lost income and other out of pocket losses. Nonfinancial damages include compensation for physical and emotional pain and suffering, mental trauma and other damages.

What are some common types of personal injury claims?

Some common forms of accidents resulting in personal injury litigation include motor vehicle accidents, premise liability claims (slip/trip and fall), dog bite/attack, nursing home abuse claims and workplace injuries.
